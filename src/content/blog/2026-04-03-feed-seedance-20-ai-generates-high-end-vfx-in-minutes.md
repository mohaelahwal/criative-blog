The visual effects (VFX) industry is experiencing a significant transformation, with artificial intelligence increasingly democratizing high-end production capabilities. A recent buzz circulating online points to Seedance 2.0 as a prime example of this paradigm shift. This development, if widely adopted and validated, suggests a profound disruption to traditional VFX pipelines, making sophisticated visual artistry accessible to a much broader audience beyond specialized studios. The ability to create such high-value assets with remarkable speed and minimal technical expertise through AI raises critical questions about workflow efficiencies, cost structures, and the future role of human artists in an increasingly automated creative landscape. Seedance 2.0 could indeed reshape how visual content is conceived and produced.
